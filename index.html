About

I was born in the Rotunda hospital, Dublin in 1999, I have spent all my life, however, in the countryside. As a kid I loved living in the countryside, I loved climbing trees and roaming around the forests in my area. This novelty started wearing off as I grew older; I started to become interested in technology and more specifically computers. With this I started to realise the down-side of the countryside: Terrible Wi-fi.At age 8 I recieved my first laptop, it was a Samsung RV511, I remember the model number as I spent well over a year researching the best specs for a budget.

My interest in computers grew progressively as I grew older and began to understand more about them and how powerful they are. I knew since 4th year in secondary school that computer science would be my ideal course because of my interest. Thus, when the dreaded Leaving cert rolled around I decided to work really hard to get enough points for Computer Science. I joined a local study centre and following daily nights of studying hard for a year, I finally sat the Leaving Cert. It went well overwall and in particular I did well in my favourite subject, Maths.

I am now in University College Cork studying Computer Science and I am really enjoying it. I chose this particular course because of the diversity in aspects of computer science it contains. There is a core programming module alongside other modules which cover different aspects of computing such as Databases and Hardware. My favourite module, however, is web development, I love learning how to create websites that both look nice and are user friendly.
